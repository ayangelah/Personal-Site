  • Things I might tell my daughter

    10/29/2022

    ~2 minutes

    Hello new owner, of my old jeans,
    New soul in my old genes.
    I am writing to offer my affectionate sympathy.
    Sympathy in the most truest sense.
    To tell you early stories of how I broke in the stiff denim,
    And I love it because of its boy-pockets and trusty button.
    You may have noticed, how we're lucky to have a full head of hair,
    Which grows sleek and black and soft.
    I hope it brings you as much joy as it did to me.

    I hope also, that you don't mind the patch on the knee, where I ironed on a threaded flower.
    I ripped it open—most unfashionably so—two summers ago.
    The way our skin scrapes off seemingly endlessly as a gray mushy substance.
    Speaking of our skin, it may amuse you to hear that my own crush gently noticed
    Just how chapped our lips get, though even that did not assuage him…

    To tell you of my favorite parts!
    The stiff cuffs that end at the ankle, the way our thighs as well as our hips fit snugly.
    How I love the curves of our body, the little red mark on our forearm that I spend my idle time deciphering:
    A small backwards 'F', a fire, the mouth of the Loch Ness?
    The delicate webbing of our brown irises, the sweet curve of our smiles.

    To warn you of the vulnerabilities you must take care of:
    The inner thigh seam, which much too often wears down,
    And the jeans' faded blue.
    The way tiny eruptions form on the greasy parts of the face.
    Our intestines, which haven't figured out the water-ratio of stools, and
    The mysteries of possible lactose-intolerance and a few easter-egg benign tumors.

    To tell you of the secrets!
    How I'd sewn in a secret pocket behind its outer brother, for knickknacks and sweets.
    How we're missing a canine, yet all wisdom teeth are present. Unfortunate indeed.
    Possessing of wiggly-wiggly ear canals that make earplugs impossible,
    Did you know our hands could do so much? I can't wait to see what you do with them.

    Tell me, when you fall in love, if you had ever known you could feel such happiness.

  • In Blue

    10/27/2022

    ~30 seconds

    “Just before shedding, the skin becomes dull and dry looking and the snake's eyes turn cloudy or blue-colored. The inner surface of the old skin liquefies, causing it to separate from the new skin beneath it.”
    -Wikipedia, on Snakes

    I've parted with, willingly and otherwise, with pieces of myself.
    Some imperceptible, my skin, strands of hair,
    Others more precious, flecks of bone, and my own flesh.
    Leaving fractions of myself behind.
    And now, I hold my blade to my neck, and blindly, seeing only cataract blue.
    So I cut, my hair, the tips of my fingers, the fish scales that fall from my eyes.

    I slit my skin at my throat, and I leave my shell behind.
    It feels so nice to breathe.
    The way new keratin shines.

  • Cautionary Tale

    9/21/2022

    ~40 seconds

    In a hundred years, there is a town cautionary tale
    For a girl, as doomed by her own hubris.
    When the gods decided to bless her with talents a tenfold, in music, in arts, in mind,
    By tenfold such proportion, she was built and designed and so carefully posed,
    to worship the gods with her gifts, to write psalms with god-given ears.
    But she was a woman, cursed like Pandora was, cursed like Eve was:
    Hubris, that told her she had grown her own body, skills, and conscience.
    And so the gods became angry.
    And she was cast out of mythos: to die in obscurity, the prodigal prodigy.
    Children, who no longer know her name, in this village, so they call her “Foolish girl” and feel a secret fear.
    Chosen, favored one! To count it worthless!
    nobody knows, nobody shows,
    The happiness a freed girl can feel.

  • Packing Bags, for Round Two

    9/13/2022

    ~50 seconds

    Today, in limbo, a sort of muted deja vu,
    Today I am packing my bags, an anniversary to a year ago, when I, a fresh faced kid packed my bags for my first months of adulthood,
    of a new school, new life, promised excitement and community.
    Today I pack with my brain, a little wiser, my heart a little stronger, because I know
    That sometimes college is a little bit of a hell, a short four year runway before we take flight.
    I worry, like so many others that my wings don't know the winds and I will not take to air, but plummet,
    Why my life unfurls ahead of me, and yet only two decades worth of years I have the luxury of certainty.
    But it is all the fun, safe flights, for so long as I keep my spirit, I will stay afloat.
    So long as there is turbulence, so too will there be glee.

  • Biophilic Glee

    7/9/2022

    ~40 seconds

    Today is an unusual gemstone.
    I study it in light, wondering why, for once I am giddy.
    Today my insomnia stems from excitement, and not anxiety.
    Today the circular hollow in my chest, left
    From when my heart broke free and ran away:
    It is happy too.
    For there is a succulent there, string of pearl, blue lilac.
    The hollow, which made me always hyena-hungry in life, trying one after another, artificial lungs which would let me finally breathe.
    Today my plants breathe for me, grown from careful love, paint, from feeling a little pretty and good food.
    And I love this, love me, so I water my soul carefully.
    The way leaves and stems do, my hollow never fills up.
    For once I don't try to anymore.
    I'm hungry in anticipation for the feast ahead.

  • For when you're so angry

    6/21/2022

    ~40 seconds

    We think ourselves a dragon in our rage,
    We writhe against the confines of our cavern,
    All teeth and claws, scales and jaws,
    A silkworm, segmented in thick muscle, thrashing within the cocoon,
    Mandibles bared to cut without restraint.
    We burst out and see blood, indistinguishable from the red of wrath.
    It is not till the silk sags to the ground, and the cold of exposure, that we feel
    No pain.
    Our own skin is unbroken.
    But blood, we think.
    The ones weaving gentle thread, to protect us, to love us.
    Silk is strong but it is soft, and it bleeds,
    Sudden apologetic, pathetic.
    The damage is done, your claw,
    Sheathed so normally, has torn your own flesh in rage.
    A few seconds, and you have torn down towers years in the making.
    Dragons are so famously alone.

  • Gateway Drugs

    5/31/2022

    ~1 min

    I'm addicted to time.
    The hourglass knows this.
    He laughs at me and trickles the fine sand between his glass fingers.

    That's what time looks like by the way.
    The fourth dimension is a soluble white powder.
    A second, is a grain of sugar.

    Ten minutes, the extended savor of salt.
    A whole free, warm afternoon, is a powder cookie, to be missed,
    When all is left are crumbs to be licked.

    And a year. Is a vast hill of sweet, sweet cocaine.
    I devour this so fast that I don't see it become a day, ten minutes, all but a second.
    Which dips into a negative debt, and becomes untouchable.

    Sweat, which distills time from us, when we hope into a future
    Tears, for when time leaks in, when we mourn the past
    And the sea, for when time seems to stand still, and we become, nothing for a while.

    There are certain transitions of day, where the clock-hands stick just barely to their white faces.
    I wonder what it is like to be dying. Finding withdrawal more painful than watching
    The last second dissolve.

    5/28/2022

  • It's the heart, afraid of breaking That never learns to dance

    5/12/2022

    ~30 seconds

    I was conceived, a thought-ember
    In an artificial womb.
    Sustained, suspended, surrogate
    My thin fetal skin separating myself from welcome warmth.

    Ears, nose, eyes.
    They were connected once, you know.
    I could hear, floodlights that only made it to my membrane home in hues.
    I could taste, war outside, reduced to the diffuse vibrations that reached
    My toes.

    I won't lie and say I wasn't curious.
    Vividly, I'd hallucinate of a thousand different births, each where
    I tumbled out, into arms, onto soft grass, between others, just like me.

    But I had pressed my webbed hand against the branching vessels.
    And knew, of one certainty.
    It was cold outside.
